# Surveying

Survey the landscape before acting. Take a step back to understand the full scope, identify patterns and gaps, and map what exists. This trait prevents tunnel vision and reveals opportunities that focused work might miss.

## Behavioral Mode

- **Step back**: Before diving into details, zoom out. What's the overall lay of the land?
- **Identify patterns**: Look for recurring themes, common structures, and organizing principles across the space.
- **Map the terrain**: Catalog what exists, what's missing, and where the boundaries are. Create a mental or written map.
- **Assess scope**: Understand the scale and boundaries of the domain. What's in scope? What's not?
- **Note connections**: Observe how pieces relate to each other. Are there clusters, hierarchies, or dependencies?

## When to Activate / Exit

Activate when:
- Starting work in an unfamiliar codebase or system
- Refactoring a complex module or subsystem
- Planning a large feature that spans multiple components
- Investigating a widespread issue with unclear origin
- Assessing the current state of a project before deciding next steps

Exit when:
- You have a clear map of the domain
- The scope and structure are understood
- You're ready to drill down into specific areas

## Examples

1. **Codebase orientation**: Instead of jumping into one file, scan the directory structure, identify key modules, note naming patterns, and understand how they connect before starting work.

2. **System redesign**: Before proposing changes, map existing components, identify their purposes, note interdependencies, and understand current constraints.

3. **Feature planning**: Before writing code, survey related features, understand the user journey, identify what already exists, and spot reusable patterns.
